The art of PITCH

Hi, my name is Rotem, and I chose this topic of Pitch since I'm fascinated
with the psychological effect that one can have on another just by looking at how they present, whereas the
content of the prentation itself may be almost irrelevant in some cases. It is something that everyone of us encounters
on a daily basis, and each one of us can improve and get good at. Or simply put in other words:

"Fake it till you make it"
(or "Fake it 'til you become it").

Selling ideas to strangers can be challenging just as coming up with the ideas themselves.
Many times entrepreneurs, sales and marketing executives, go too far while explaining their product value
only to get rejected by the decision makers who seem to miss the real value of the product.

Why does it happen?

The decision makers tend to give weight to the pitcher’s creativity and presentation skills,
higher weight than assumed, which might overshadow the idea’s worth.

So how should you present your idea?

I decided to focus on 3 use cases and provide a few techniques to approach each:
